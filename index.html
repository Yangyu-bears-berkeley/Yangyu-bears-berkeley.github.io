About me

 

      I am currently a postdoc with Berkeley Education Alliance for Resaerch in Singapore Limited (BEARS), University of California, Berkeley, supervised by Prof. Costas J. Spanos (UC Berkeley) and Prof. Guoqiang Hu (Nanyang Technological University).

Prior to that I got my PhD. from Tsinghua University, supervised by Prof. Xiaohong Guan and Prof. (Samual)Qing-Shan Jia.

My research focuses on developing algorithmic solutions to advance a flexible, sustainable and reliable energy system. I have integrated interdisciplinary theories and techniques such as stochastic optimization, reinforcement learning, decentralized optimization, game theory and market design mechanisms to address the challenges faced by the current energy systems: increasing demand, low energy efficiency, emerging renewable and energy storage. This requires developing principled control mechanism to achieve energy system coordination.

I have worked under the guidance of Prof. Costas J. Spanos (UCB) and Prof. Guoqiang Hu (NTU), and have achieved significant progress in the optimization and control of energy systems at both equipment and system integration level. At equipment level, we have developed computationally efficient control method to facilitate energy-efficient heating, venting and air-conditioning (HVAC) systems for providing comfortable indoor environment. At system integration level, we have designed various business models to advance the penetration of energy storage (ES) and renewable energy (RE) technologies in the grid.

      My research interests are in the area of cyber-physical systems, such as smart grid and smart buildings from the perspective of market mechanism design, optimization and control. The goal is to achieve a principled coordination of the complex dynamic systems with the integrated interdisciplinary theories and techniques such as decentralized control, reinforcement learning (RL) , game theory and market mechanisms.

Academic Experience & Programs

Programs

  1. 2018-present: Building Efficiency and Sustainability in the Tropics 2 (NRF of Singapore, headed by Prof. Costas J. Spanos)
    Theme B. Smart Technologies for Agile, Intelligent, Efficient and Resilient Buildings

    • Develop an adaptive controller for ACMV system to support the diverse and uncertain demand of occupants

    • Develop a scalabe (decentralized) controller for large multi-zone ACMV systems to achieve comfortable indoor environment quality ( i.e., thermal comfort and indoor air quality (IAQ))

    • Experimental resolution and evaluation of control methods for buildings’ ACMV system on Test-bed

    • Develop a proximal linearization-based decentralized method for nonconvex problems with general nonlinear constraints

  2. 2015-2018: Modeling and Simulation of Energy Internet (EI) (National Key R&D Project of China)

    • Data-driven analysis of electric vehicle (EV) charging demand

    • Establish joint scheduling and simulation model to coordinate EV charging with distributed renewable generation in building micro-grids

    • Develop simulation-based distributed policy improvement method to help improve from heuristic charging policies

    • Develop EV-based decentralized charging policy to achieve dynamic balance of demand and supply in building micro-grids

  3. 2015-2018: Multi-scale policy optimization of networked discrete event dynamic system (National Natural Science Foundation of China)

    • Establish a hierarchical scheduling and simulation model to coordinate stochastic EV charging demand and the uncertain distributed renewable generation in building micro-grid

    • Proposed a two-level resolution to optimize the EV charging policies

  4. 2014-2015: Data-Driven Analysis of Energy Consumption of Occupant (Support in part by National Natural Science Foundation of China)

    • Build a platform to collect real-time information (i.e., location, energy consumption) of individual occupants

    • Proposed a data-driven model to improve the prediction accuracy of occupant’s energy consumption
